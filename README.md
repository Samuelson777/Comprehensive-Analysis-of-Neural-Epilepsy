## 📌 Overview
This repository hosts the interactive digital publication of the conceptual research paper, **"Neural Epilepsy: Bridging Silicon and Synapse."** Traditional research papers often trap complex, multi-dimensional data in static PDFs. This project translates neurobiological research, clinical efficacy data, and qualitative patient surveys into an accessible, interactive Single-Page Application (SPA). It explores the paradigm shift from traditional pharmacotherapy to advanced neural circuit modulation and AI-driven diagnostics.

## ✨ Key Features
* **Interactive Data Visualization:** Dynamic Doughnut and Bar charts (powered by Chart.js) detailing global etiology and intervention efficacy rates.
* **Thematic Qualitative Analysis:** Tabbed interfaces organizing 500+ patient survey results into Quality of Life, Cognitive Impact, and Social Dynamics.
* **Clinical Pathway Flow:** A stepped visual hierarchy mapping the critical decision-making process from initial pharmacotherapy to neurosurgery.
* **Ethical Considerations Accordion:** Expandable sections addressing data privacy, BCI telemetry, and informed consent.
* **Fully Responsive Design:** Built with Tailwind CSS to ensure readability across mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.

## 🔬 Research Conclusion
The research concludes that the future of epilepsy management is not found in a single pharmacological pill, but in a multidisciplinary synergy.

By treating the "Neural" in epilepsy—understanding the brain simultaneously as a biological circuit subject to channelopathies and as a computational tool capable of interfacing with advanced telemetry—we can fundamentally alter patient outcomes. The transition from systemic, side-effect-heavy polytherapy to targeted neuromodulation (RNS/DBS) and AI-driven predictive forecasting marks the dawn of precision epileptology. Ultimately, bridging silicon and synapse provides the most viable roadmap to closing the 50-90% global treatment gap and restoring autonomy to the millions living with drug-resistant neural epilepsy.
